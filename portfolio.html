Projector Lens Black & White

Project Description:

This series explores experimental black and white photography using vintage projector lenses adapted for modern cameras. These lenses were never designed for still photography, producing unusual optical characteristics such as soft focus falloff, swirling bokeh, and unpredictable depth.

By removing colour, the images focus entirely on contrast, texture, and light. The imperfections of the projector lenses become part of the artistic process, creating atmospheric and timeless results.

Techniques Used:

  • Adapted vintage projector lenses
  • Manual focus experimentation
  • High contrast black and white processing
  • Emphasis on texture and tonal range
Japanese Photography Inspiration

Project Description:

This series draws inspiration from Japanese photography, particularly its emphasis on mood, simplicity, and quiet observation. Rather than dramatic moments, the work focuses on subtle details, fleeting light, and everyday environments that evoke atmosphere.

The images aim to create a sense of stillness and contemplation through minimalist composition, negative space, and attention to transient moments.

Inspiration & Approach:

  • Minimalist composition and negative space
  • Observation of everyday environments
  • Use of light to create mood
  • Atmosphere over narrative
Japanese Influenced Nature

Project Description:

This collection explores nature through a photographic approach influenced by Japanese visual philosophy. The images focus on harmony, balance, and the relationship between subject and environment rather than dramatic spectacle.

Close attention is given to subtle textures, natural patterns, and small moments that might otherwise go unnoticed, presenting nature as something intimate and quietly expressive.

Inspiration & Process:

  • Observation of small natural details
  • Balanced and uncluttered composition
  • Soft natural lighting
  • Calm visual rhythm and harmony
